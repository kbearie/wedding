Travel

We’re so excited to celebrate with everyone in Durango. As you join us for our wedding weekend, we hope you’ll also have time to experience and explore this beautiful part of Colorado. Below are a few travel, lodging, dining, and activity recommendations to help make your trip easy and fun.

Travel

Durango–La Plata County Airport

The Durango airport is about 20 minutes from downtown Durango. There are nonstop flights from Dallas, Denver, and Phoenix, with seasonal nonstop flights to Houston.

Where to Stay

The Strater Hotel

Located on Main St. in downtown Durango, The Strater Hotel has 88 unique guest rooms, a Wild West themed saloon, a craft cocktail lounge, a steak and seafood restaurant, and year-round live entertainment for all to enjoy. Durango’s history comes alive at the Historic Strater Hotel.

Website

The DoubleTree by Hilton

The DoubleTree Hotel is a casually elegant hotel with 150 rooms located on the Animas River in downtown Durango. It has an indoor pool, hot tub, and on-site restaurant.

Website

The Rochester Hotel

The Rochester Hotel is a romantic boutique hotel located in downtown Durango. It is a historic landmark that blends history and contemporary elegance. This newly renovated property has 15 rooms and a beautiful bar and garden area on site.

Website

Outdoorsy Campground

For guests looking for a unique stay, this charming campground is perfectly situated between Downtown Durango and Flying B Ranch. Offering both RV sites and luxury glamping tents, it’s a peaceful escape along the scenic Pine River.

Website

Where to Dine

Steamworks

One of Durango’s best restaurants, Steamworks has great food and a fun atmosphere. There is a great beer selection and a large menu with a variety of options. Located in downtown Durango and family friendly, this is a great stop for everyone.

Website

Diamond Belle Saloon

The Diamond Belle Saloon is a historic Wild West saloon that offers food and drinks, along with live music from talented musicians every night.

Website

Eolus

Known for exceptional food and service, Eolus offers a fresh take on New American cuisine. They source many ingredients from local farms and producers, and every dish is full of flavor and authenticity.

Website

The Nugget Mountain Bar

The Nugget Mountain Bar is located half a mile south of Purgatory Ski Resort. The bar is an old miner’s cabin with a large deck and views of the San Juan Mountains, offering casual food and drinks with a beautiful view.

Website

Things to Do

Hike Animas Mountain

A popular and iconic trail in Durango, Animas Mountain has spectacular views and the main loop is easy to follow. The loop is a lollipop heading in a counter-clockwise direction. Hike up the trail for a few hundred yards from the parking area and take a right at the first trail junction, continuing to follow the loop counter-clockwise.

More Info

Durango + Silverton Narrow Gauge Historic Train

The Durango and Silverton Narrow Gauge Railroad winds through spectacular and breathtaking canyons in the remote wilderness of the San Juan National Forest. Experience the same tracks that miners, cowboys, and settlers of the Old West took.

More Info

Durango Hot Springs

Durango Hot Springs is located 8 miles north of downtown Durango and 15 miles south of Purgatory Ski Resort. Surrounded by mountain views, these natural mineral hot springs are the perfect place to relax. This newly renovated resort also offers massages and spa treatments.

More Info

Purgatory Ski Resort

Purgatory Ski Resort offers many activities. In the summer they offer a mountain coaster, alpine slide, treasure panning, a scenic chairlift ride, and paddle boarding.
